Simple Moving Average

Simple Moving Average is a trading algorithm that uses previous trends in the data to calculate trades. It does so by keeping up with two averages, a twenty day average and a five day average. If the five day average is higher than the twenty day average some of the coin is sold, with the assumption that it has reached a "high" value. When the five day average is lower than the twenty day average some of the coin is bought under the assumption that the coin has reached a "low" value. This way the algorithm achieves the "buy high, sell low" that is needed for profitable trading.

Value Averaging

Value Averaging is a trading strategy that pits different parts of the market against each other to create trades. This algorithm keeps commodities in several different areas, or in our case coins, of the market and maintains equal value in each commodity. This way is the value of a single coin goes up against the market some of it is divested into other coins which is how "selling high" is achieved in this algorithm. If the value of a coin falls agains the rest of the market then some other coins' values are transfered over into this coind which is how "selling low" is achieved.

Momentum

Momentum strategy algorithms looks at what the market has previously done and assumes that it will continue to do so. In this case when the value of a coin has been rising more of the coin is purchased under the assumption that it will continue to rise. If the value of a coin has been decreasing then it is sold under the assumption that it will continue to decrease in value. This is not a "buy high, sell low", style of trading algorithm, more of a "buy on the rise, sell on the fall agorithm."
